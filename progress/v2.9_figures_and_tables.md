## Figures Generated

### Figure 2: Orthogonality Scatter Plot
- 4 conditions: Clean, Noise Only, OOD Only, Noise+OOD
- Shows clear clustering: noise increases u_a (x-axis), OOD increases u_e (y-axis)
- Threshold lines at tau_a=0.3, tau_e=0.7
- Orthogonality stats: Pearson |r|=0.004, Spearman=0.004

### Figure 3: Behavioral Isolation
- 2 panels: Noise Sweep (u_a rises, u_e flat) and OOD Sweep (u_e rises, u_a flat)
- Demonstrates estimators respond independently to their respective perturbation types

### Figure 4: Noise Level Sweep (Table 2 visualization)
- Line plot: 5 noise levels × 6 methods
- Shows Decomposed/Multi-Sample robust while Vanilla degrades, DE/MCD worse than vanilla

### Figure 5: OOD Comparison (Table 3 visualization)
- Grouped bar chart: 6 OOD scenarios × 6 methods
- Clear visual showing DE/MCD failure and Decomposed advantage over Total Uncertainty

### Figure 6: Intervention Breakdown
- Left: Pie chart showing intervention types under noise (90.3% filter, 9.6% combined, 0.1% conservative)
- Right: Stacked bar per OOD scenario showing increasing combined interventions under OOD

### Figure 8: Conformal Coverage
- 2 panels: Coverage guarantee (both methods hit target) and Abstention cost
- Decomposed CP: ~2.5% abstention vs Total Uncertainty CP: ~94% abstention

### Figure 9: t-SNE Visualization
- 36-dim observation space projected to 2D
- 4 conditions show distinct clusters, validating that perturbations create separable observation distributions
